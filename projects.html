Art

I've been creating and crafting things of one kind or another for much of my life. The more memorable of those have nearly always been both conceived and delivered as gifts, and hence have never accumulated in one place.

Riverside Church cross

A cross put together from reclaimed pallet planks, nailed together and shaped with a circular saw.

May-October 2021; about 7' and 200lb.

Hollowed-book purse

A fancy edition of Gulliver's Travels remade into a fancy handbag for a Jonathan Swift fan.

2018, for Anila.

Book-tree

Paper arrangement: a coiled wire tree covered in paper strips set on a book with a dremel-shaped slope, with a paper bench and swing, and a brass watch movement in place of a sun.

2018, for Beth.

Cutlery flowers

Metal cutlery heated, bent, wound & glued together into flowers.

2018, for Stacy.

Sermon-covers

Wire base looped with thread, ornamenting a pastor's sermon-booklet.

For Heather, 2018.

Framed tree

A coiled wire tree set in/out of a picture frame, with miniature wire outdoor-furniture in its presumed shade.

2017, for Daniela.

Urania moth

Thread wound over metal wire frame, modeled on a Urania moth.

2016, for Leona.

Shard tree

Coiled wire and pretty crystals shaped into a more figurative tree than my usual ones.

For Tamar, 2016.

Willow tree

Coiled wire and stone beads tree set on a rock; went way over-ambitious on that one--possibly as much as a thousand of the tiny stone-leaves there.

2012, for Nitzan.

Red fox moth

Wire frame wrapped with thread and fuzzy yarn.

For Tanya, 2012.

Pop-up butterfly

Wire, screw, metal cigarette box. A cable system folded the flower down when the lid closed and hauled it back straight when opened, though it couldn't close all the way, and the mechanism wore out soon enough so I eventually just fixed it in the open position.

2011, for Margalit.

Bony rabbit

Articulated rabbit skeleton set on a tree base next to a sheep skull. Its tiny wrists were especially a bear to put together--definitely inspired a respect for just how exquisitely crafted we all are.

2011.

Broken

About as close as I've ever gotten to concept art, a car-victim jackal skull set by broken glass.

2010.

Late tree

Coiled wire and stone beads tree set on a rock; took a long break in the middle of that project so it was technically several years in the making.

Finished in 2010, for Mom.

Turtle

Sea turtle skull, sea shells on a wire frame, and barbed-wire flippers mounted with counterweights for a gentle bobbing motion. Originally conceived as a present for Dafi, but I eventually realized that there's a size limit to acceptable gifts; Dafi got a bobble-head cat skull instead.

2010, for Dafi.

Dragonfly

An antique sextant as a setting for a wire-crafted dragonfly.

2007, for Masha.
